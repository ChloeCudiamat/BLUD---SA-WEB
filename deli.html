Pure delicacies from these provinces.. Either the best from them, made by them, or just something they're known for.

Bulacan Delicacies

Pastillas

Soft, sweet milk candies often wrapped in colorful paper, a signature treat from Bulacan that melts in your mouth.

Empanada

A golden, crispy pastry stuffed with savory meat, vegetables, or cheese, perfect for a quick snack or meal on the go.

Chicharon

Crunchy, fried pork skin seasoned to perfection, a beloved snack or pulutan for gatherings.

Minasa

A soft, buttery, and slightly crumbly cookie from Bulacan, often enjoyed with coffee or hot chocolate. Its delicate texture melts in your mouth, making it a classic local treat.

Inipit

A sweet snack made of two thin layers of soft pastry filled with rich, creamy custard or ube. Its name means “pressed together,” perfectly describing its delicious layered treat.

La Union Delicacies

Halo-Halo de Iloko

A colorful mix of shaved ice, sweet beans, fruits, and creamy leche flan.

Pinakbet

A hearty mix of vegetables cooked in shrimp paste, showcasing Ilocano flavors.

Bagnet

Deep-fried pork belly with an ultra-crispy skin and tender inside, perfect as a main dish or pulutan.

Longganisa

Garlicky and savory sausages, a breakfast favorite that’s packed with flavor.

Kakanin

Sweet sticky rice treats, perfect for dessert or a quick snack.

Pangasinan Delicacies

Bangus

Known far and wide, Pangasinan's milkfish is celebrated for its exceptional taste and quality, making it a local favorite.

Pigar-Pigar

A savory beef dish, thinly sliced and quickly stir-fried to perfection, providing a delightful culinary experience.

Tupig

A grilled rice cake made from glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and brown sugar, wrapped in banana leaves, giving it a smoky and sweet flavor.

Binungey

Rice cooked in bamboo tubes, offering a unique and aromatic flavor, often enjoyed as a snack or dessert.

Bocayo

Sweetened coconut strips, a simple yet delightful treat made from young coconut meat and sugar, perfect for those with a sweet tooth.
